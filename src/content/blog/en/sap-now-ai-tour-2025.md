Hi, this is Rabbit! 🐰

On July 15, **SAP NOW AI Tour 2025 Korea** was held at the Grand InterContinental Seoul Parnas. Pre-registration alone topped 2,500 people, and the Grand Ballroom where the keynote was held filled up early. I ended up watching the keynote from a separate overflow room on a large screen — and that room had no empty seats either. Just trying to find a seat gave me an early sense of how much interest the industry has in SAP and business AI right now.

![Wide shot of the SAP NOW AI Tour 2025 Korea venue. A large screen displays the event logo, and every round table is packed with attendees](/images/sap-now-ai-tour-hall.jpg)
*Figure 1. A packed venue with no empty seats. The interest in SAP and business AI was something I felt in the density of the room, not just the numbers.*

## The question the keynote raised

The morning keynote's theme was "Unleash Your Future with Business AI." SAP's global Chief People Officer and the President of Data & Analytics took the stage themselves to talk about how AI moves beyond a technical demo and delivers results inside real business processes. Partner booths from AWS, Microsoft, and others pointed in the same direction. The whole venue felt aligned around a single question: **"how do you actually put AI to work in the business?"**

## What SAP calls AI: Joule

The word that came up most in the keynote was **Joule**. It's the AI assistant SAP has built into S/4HANA, and when you give it a plain-language instruction — "clean up this quarter's open orders" — it works across systems to produce a result. SAP positioned it not as a simple chatbot, but as AI that actually processes transactions within business workflows.

What stood out was how they framed it. The emphasis wasn't on "how smart is it," but on "what work does it actually change." Automated recommendations for journal entries, optimizing the purchase approval process, sharper demand forecasting — this was a session built on concrete business cases rather than flashy demos.

## What I confirmed in Track 3

In the afternoon, I focused on Track 3, "Business Suite with AI," out of the four available tracks. Session after session converged on the same point: for Joule to actually work, it needs a Clean Core foundation, and getting to Clean Core means cleaning up the custom code that's piled up in the system first. It was an afternoon where the appeal of the new feature and the weight of the prerequisite needed to adopt it were both equally present.

## Walking out of the venue

What stuck with me walking out wasn't the name of some new feature — it was a single question.

> **"I want to use Joule — but is our system actually ready to receive it?"**

The weight of that reality, sitting behind the flashy demo, was heavier than I expected. I've laid out that thinking in the post below.
