Hi, this is Rabbit! 🐰

Imagine a chef's exam changing from written to practical. Where you once memorized "how many grams of salt?" and answered multiple choice, now you step into the kitchen and actually cook.

The SAP certification exam changed the same way. The confusingly worded multiple-choice questions are gone, replaced by a format where you enter a real system and complete tasks directly. Today I'll walk through how the new exam actually runs — and the traps that can sink you if you don't know them.

## The exam splits into two types

The new SAP exam comes in two types. One important point: you can't choose between them. The nature of the certification subject already determines which exam you take.

- **System-based exam**: Development/configuration subjects. You connect to a real SAP server and operate it directly.
- **AI scenario exam**: Consulting/explanatory subjects. You're given a customer situation and propose solutions in dialogue with an AI.

In kitchen terms, a cooking certificate makes you actually cook (hands-on), while a manager certificate role-plays a customer-service situation. Both give you up to three hours, and you can sit the same subject four times a year.

## System-based exam: into the real kitchen

This exam isn't a simulated screen — you connect to a real SAP cloud server to complete tasks. Depending on the module, the actual environment opens up: SAP GUI, the Fiori launchpad, BTP Cockpit, even Eclipse for development subjects.

The exam consists of about eight large exercises, each containing detailed sub-tasks. You get three hours on paper, but with solid hands-on knowledge it's a 40-minute-to-one-hour job. The grading criterion is "can you go into the menus and configure the instance as guided," so for anyone who has practiced, there are no traps — it's actually straightforward.

The flow is: 'Enroll' → review instructions → 'Access Practice System' to launch the server → complete the tasks. But there are two traps you absolutely must know.

> ⚠️ **Trap 1 — the `Confirm completion` button**: This button at the bottom doesn't mean 'one task done' — it means **'final submission of the entire exam.'** Press it thinking you've finished one task and the exam ends, with no way back. Press it only after you've finished every exercise.

> ⚠️ **Trap 2 — turn off browser auto-translate**: Exam text and system values are graded in English only. Turn on your browser's Korean translation and SAP's own menu names and instance names get mistranslated, which can cause configuration errors or break the grading engine. You must sit the exam with the original English intact.

## AI scenario exam: a consulting role-play with AI

Consulting and sales-oriented subjects use this format. Given a business situation (a use case) — say, a company's S/4HANA adoption — it evaluates how well you propose and guide as a consultant.

There are two options, and the live role-play with an AI avatar is the one to choose. Recording your own presentation for review takes about four weeks to grade. The AI option lets you sit it immediately, with no time booking.

And here's a point that matters especially for beginners. Unlike the system-based exam, the AI scenario exam fully supports Korean. The AI avatar asks in Korean and the captions come through cleanly, so even if English feels daunting, you can answer in Korean. The AI isn't a tape recorder — it understands the context of your answers and asks natural follow-ups. One thing: keep your camera on throughout.

## After the exam: results and the certificate

For both exams, you can check pass/fail within about 15 minutes of submitting, in the 'Review' tab, because grading is automatic.

If you pass, within two weeks you'll get an email from Credly — a global certification-management site — about issuing your digital badge. SAP doesn't hand you a paper certificate directly; you receive a digital badge and a PDF certificate through Credly. You can share that badge straight to social media like LinkedIn.

## So how do you prepare?

Since it's a practical exam, memorizing past questions doesn't work, and no sample questions are provided. So preparation really comes down to one thing: SAP Learning Hub.

Inside the Learning Hub are the same practice servers as the real exam, plus hands-on guide materials. Repeating the guide's steps until they're second nature is the surest path — essentially rehearsing the exact environment you'll face. For whether to pursue the certification at all and what it costs, see [The SAP certification guide](/en/blog/sap-certification).

## Rabbit's Takeaway

"It switched to practical, so it must be harder" is the most common misconception. The reality is the opposite. The old multiple-choice exam had many trick questions twisted to confuse you; now it honestly checks just one thing — "can you do it in the system as guided?"

It shifted from a test you memorize and guess at, to a test that people who've actually done it pass. So the direction of prep is clear too: not a question bank, but moving your hands on the practice server. Remember that one thing and the exam looks far more manageable. 😎
