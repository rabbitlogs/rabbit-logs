Hi, this is Rabbit! 🐰

To work in a restaurant, you need a food-safety certificate. But in some places that certificate has to be renewed every year, and if you miss the renewal, you start the training over from scratch.

The SAP certification now works the same way. The era of "pass once, keep it for life" is over; today it's closer to a membership you maintain every year. Before you start, you need to understand the cost structure clearly to avoid regret. Here's the whole picture.

> **In 3 lines**
> - SAP exam eligibility comes through four routes: formal training, government/university programs, subscription, or verified experience.
> - Training cost (millions of won) is the largest piece; the exam fee itself is relatively small.
> - Since 2024, you must pass a yearly 'Stay Certified' assessment to keep the certification valid.

## First, are you even eligible to sit the exam?

You can't just register for an SAP exam directly. You need to earn eligibility first, through one of four main routes.

- **Formal training**: Completing an academy course at an SAP-authorized training partner. The most reliable path — costly, but it gives you structured learning and exam eligibility at once.
- **Government or university programs**: State-funded courses (like Korea's K-Digital Training) or university-linked programs. Lower cost, popular with students and job seekers.
- **SAP Learning Hub subscription**: Subscribing to SAP's official online learning platform, which provides materials and exam eligibility together.
- **Verified experience**: If you have 6+ months of relevant experience at an SAP customer or partner, you can submit proof of employment and a work history to get approved.

> 💡 **Key**: Most people go through the 'formal training' or 'subscription' route. Earning eligibility through self-study alone is, in practice, very difficult.

## The real cost is training, not the exam fee

Let's talk about the most practical part: money. An SAP certification has three cost pieces, and training is by far the biggest.

Authorized training costs vary by module but usually run in the range of several million won. To target the S/4HANA production planning (PP) certification (`C_TS422`), for example, you'd start with the related academy course.

The exam fee, by contrast, is small next to training. By SAP's official pricing, a two-attempt bundle is **about $276** (a 12-month contract). If you only picture "the cost of taking the exam once," you'll badly underestimate the real burden. The training is what opens your wallet.

There are rules, too. You can sit the same exam up to four times a year, and after a fail you can retake it 24 hours later. But fail all four and you wait a full year. And once you confirm a booking, it can't be canceled or changed — so book carefully.

## The real trap is the maintenance cost

This is the most important part. An SAP certification used to be a shield you kept for life, but the rules changed completely in April 2024.

Because SAP's products evolve so fast, every certification holder must now pass a short yearly assessment to keep it valid. And to take that assessment, an **SAP Learning Hub subscription is required**. So maintaining a certification has two conditions, both of which must be met (an AND condition).

- **Step 1 — Learning Hub subscription**: The prerequisite for accessing the assessment. This subscription fee is, in effect, the maintenance cost.
- **Step 2 — Pass the yearly assessment**: A short online quiz. Pass it and your validity extends 12 months.

The yearly assessment isn't much of a burden, actually. It's an open-book quiz you can retake an unlimited number of times. You do ultimately need a perfect score to renew, but it's less a test to fail you than a check that you've kept up with the latest knowledge. The catch is simply that it requires the paid Learning Hub subscription.

> ⚠️ **Note**: Miss the renewal window and the certification **expires** — to use it again, you take the full exam from scratch.

## Already certified? You don't need to re-take it

With the recent shift to a practical, hands-on exam, many people worry, "do I have to re-earn the certification I already have?" The answer is no. Certifications you've already earned remain valid. As long as you pass the yearly assessment (Stay Certified) above, your certification stays current.

For reference, exam vouchers you bought earlier still work for the new exam. The booking path has changed with the new format, though, so for how the exam actually runs, see [How the SAP certification exam works](/en/blog/sap-exam-format).

## So — do you actually need it?

Once you know the costs and conditions, the decision gets harder. In the end it depends on your career stage.

It's worth it in these cases. For **job seekers and juniors**, with no hands-on experience yet, it's solid proof that you've prepared. For **career changers**, it's an entry ticket into SAP consulting. For **partner-firm consultants**, the company often requires it to win projects and keep its tier — so there's little choice.

Be more careful in these cases. For **experienced practitioners**, a track record of successful projects is a far stronger weapon than a certification. A certification doesn't guarantee a raise. And if **the cost is a strain**, that money might be better spent learning another skill or building real experience.

## Rabbit's Takeaway

An SAP certification is no longer a single line on a résumé. It's become a 'membership in your own expertise' — a promise of continued learning and investment. Like renewing a restaurant's hygiene grade each year, earning it is the start, not the finish.

So change the question. Instead of "should I get it or not," ask "am I at a stage where I'll maintain this membership every year?" That one shift makes the answer much clearer. 😎
