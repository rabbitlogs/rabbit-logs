Working at a restaurant requires a food hygiene certificate. At some places, though, you have to renew that certification every year, and if you miss the renewal window, you have to redo the training from scratch.

SAP certification has changed to work the same way. The era of "earn it once, keep it for life" is over — now it's closer to a membership you maintain every year. You need to understand the cost structure clearly before you commit, so there are no regrets later. Here's the full picture.

> **3-line summary**
> - SAP certification eligibility comes through four paths: formal training, government-subsidized programs, subscription, or verified work experience.
> - Training cost (in the millions of KRW) is the biggest expense; the exam fee itself is comparatively small.
> - Since 2024, you must pass an annual "Stay Certified" assessment to keep your certification valid.

## Four ways to become eligible to take the exam

Not just anyone can walk up and take an SAP exam. You first need to qualify. There are broadly four ways to do this.

- **Formal training completion**: completing an academy course at an SAP-authorized training provider — the most reliable path. Costly, but it covers structured learning and exam eligibility in one shot.
- **Government-subsidized or university training**: state-funded programs like K-Digital Training, or university-affiliated programs. Lower cost, so students and job seekers use this route often.
- **SAP Learning Hub subscription**: subscribing to SAP's official online learning platform, which grants both access to materials and exam eligibility.
- **Verified work experience**: if you have six months or more of relevant experience at an SAP customer or partner company, you can get approved by submitting an employment certificate and a description of your work experience.

> 💡 **Key point**: Most people go through the "formal training" or "subscription" route. Getting eligibility through self-study alone is practically difficult.

## The real cost driver isn't the exam fee — it's training

Let's talk about the most practical part: cost. SAP certification cost breaks into three chunks, and training cost is overwhelmingly the largest.

Authorized training cost varies by module but typically runs **around 3–5 million KRW**. For example, preparing for the [S/4HANA](https://www.sap.com/products/erp/s4hana.html) Production Planning (PP) certification (`C_TS422`) means starting with the related academy course.

The exam fee itself, by comparison, is small. Per SAP's official pricing, a **bundle of 2 exam attempts runs about 276 USD** (on a 12-month contract). If you only think about "the cost of taking one exam," it's easy to badly underestimate the real financial commitment. Training is where your wallet actually opens.

There are rules around attempts too. You can take the same exam up to 4 times a year, and you can retake it 24 hours after a fail. If you fail all 4 attempts, though, you have to wait a year before trying again. And once a booking is confirmed, it can't be cancelled or changed, so apply carefully.

## The annual maintenance fee — how it works

This is the most important part. SAP certification used to last a lifetime once earned, but starting in April 2024, the rules changed completely.

Because SAP products evolve quickly, every certification holder now has to pass a short annual assessment to keep their certification valid. And to take that assessment, an SAP Learning Hub subscription is required. Two conditions must both be met to maintain your certification (an AND condition).

- **Step 1 — Learning Hub subscription**: a prerequisite for accessing the assessment. This subscription fee effectively becomes your certification maintenance cost.
- **Step 2 — Passing the annual assessment**: a short online theory quiz. Passing extends validity by 12 months.

This annual assessment isn't a heavy lift — it's an open-book quiz you can retake unlimited times if you get something wrong. That said, you need a perfect score to complete the renewal. It's not designed to fail people; it's closer to a check that your knowledge is current. The key point is that this assessment requires a paid Learning Hub subscription to access.

> ⚠️ **Note**: If you don't pass the assessment within the required window, your certification becomes **Expired**, and you'd need to take the exam from scratch to use it again.

## If you already hold a certification

With SAP exams recently shifting to a hands-on, practical format, many people worry: "do I need to retake the certification I earned before?" The short answer is no. Certifications you've already earned remain valid as-is. As long as you pass the annual assessment (Stay Certified) described above, your certification stays valid.

Exam vouchers you purchased previously can still be used for the new exam format too. That said, since the exam format changed, the application process is different — you can find the details on how the actual exam runs now in [SAP certification exams: from a written test to a hands-on kitchen exam room](/en/blog/sap-exam-format).

## Who actually needs certification

Once you understand the cost and requirements, the decision gets more nuanced. In the end, it comes down to your career stage.

Recommended cases: for job seekers and new grads without hands-on experience, it's solid proof of "here's how much I've prepared." For career changers, it acts as an entry ticket into working as an SAP consultant. For partner-company consultants, the company often requires it to win projects or maintain partner tier, leaving little choice in the matter.

On the flip side, there are cases worth being cautious about. For experienced professionals, project success stories are a far stronger asset than a certification. Certification doesn't guarantee a raise. If the cost is a real burden, that money might be better spent learning a different skill or gaining hands-on experience.

## Rabbit's Takeaway

SAP certification is no longer just a line on a resume — it's become a "membership in your own expertise" that requires ongoing learning and investment. Like renewing a restaurant's hygiene rating every year, earning it isn't the finish line — it's the start.

So reframe the question, and the decision gets easier. Instead of "should I get certified or not," ask "am I at a stage where I can keep up this membership every year?" That one shift in framing makes the answer much clearer. 😎
